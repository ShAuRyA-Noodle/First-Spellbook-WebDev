Wrath of the Crimson Wyrm

For centuries, the city of Valmere had defied the fury of the Crimson Wyrm, Zareth. But defiance had its price. The skies blackened as the beast descended, his roar splitting the heavens. The people screamed as fire rained upon them, consuming stone and steel alike. The king’s soldiers met him at the gates, their lances trembling in their hands. 

“You should have paid tribute,” Zareth growled, his voice a storm of rage. “Now, I take everything.”

The king’s champion, Sir Dain, stood alone upon the castle wall, his great sword gleaming with runes of power. “Then come and take it, beast,” he roared. 

Zareth answered with a torrent of flame, turning the wall to molten rock. But Dain did not fall. He leaped, impossibly high, meeting the dragon’s jaws with his blade. The enchanted steel sliced through scales, drawing forth a bellow of pain. The dragon thrashed, his tail smashing through towers, his wings scattering men like leaves. 

Dain struck again, carving a deep wound along Zareth’s side, but the beast’s fury was boundless. Claws raked across the knight’s armor, crushing bone beneath steel. He fell, blood staining the ruined city beneath him. 

Victory was near. 

Yet as Zareth reared back to finish him, something stirred in the dying knight’s eyes—a flicker of defiance, a final act of wrath. With his last breath, he drove his sword deep into the dragon’s chest. The runes ignited, channeling the last of his soul into the blade. A pulse of light erupted from the wound, and Zareth shrieked. His massive body convulsed, fire leaking from his wounds, his own magic consuming him from within. 

The beast crashed into the ruins, the earth shaking with his fall. His wings lay broken, his fire reduced to embers. 

With the last of his strength, Zareth turned his gaze to the knight who had undone him. “You… win…” he snarled, before the final breath left his body. 

The dragon was dead. The city was in ruins. And in the silence that followed, only the whispers of legend remained—of the man who defied a god and the beast who met his match in fury.
