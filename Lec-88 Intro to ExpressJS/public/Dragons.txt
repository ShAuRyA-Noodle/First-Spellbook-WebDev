The Last Guardian

The kingdom of Eldoria had long been protected by the last of the great dragons, Valtor. His wings cast a shadow over the castle each evening, a reminder that as long as he soared, no enemy dared threaten the land. But Valtor was old, his scales dulled, his fire no longer as fierce. And in the mountains beyond, darkness stirred.

King Aldric, fearing the dragon’s waning strength, called upon the bravest knight in the kingdom—Sir Rowan. “Find the dragon’s heir,” the king commanded. “For when Valtor falls, so shall Eldoria.”

Rowan set out, climbing the jagged cliffs where Valtor made his roost. He found the ancient beast perched upon a rocky ledge, gazing into the setting sun.

“I know why you have come,” Valtor rumbled. His voice, though weary, still held power. “But there are no others. I am the last.”

Rowan’s heart sank. “Then Eldoria is doomed.”

Valtor regarded him for a long moment before speaking. “Not all dragons are born with scales and wings.”

Confused, Rowan stepped closer. Valtor extended a trembling claw and touched the knight’s chest. In that instant, a searing heat flooded Rowan’s veins, and a vision consumed him. He saw fire, wings, power unlike any he had known. And then, he understood.

“You…” Rowan gasped. “You are passing your soul to me.”

Valtor nodded. “The fire must live on. Protect Eldoria, not with scales, but with heart.”

As the last light of the sun vanished, so too did Valtor. His body crumbled into embers that swirled around Rowan, seeping into him, igniting his spirit. He fell to his knees, feeling the power surge through his blood.

When Rowan returned, the kingdom beheld not a knight, but a guardian. His eyes glowed like molten gold, his breath carried the heat of a dragon’s flame. Enemies who once feared Valtor now trembled at the sight of the warrior who bore his essence.

Eldoria was safe. The dragon had not died.

He had simply been reborn.
